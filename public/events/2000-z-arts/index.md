**Z-arts** (originally Zion Arts Centre) is a multi-arts venue serving young people and families in Hulme, Moss Side, and Whalley Range.

## The Building

The venue at 335 Stretford Road occupies a building dating to **1911**, originally constructed as a congregational church for Zion Institute—an educational organisation whose principles were based on the teachings of Jesus Christ.

## Notable History

Before becoming an arts centre, the building hosted remarkable events:

- **1960s**: Hosted the Hallé Orchestra and Northern Ballet Theatre
- **1960s**: Luciano Pavarotti rehearsed there before a Manchester concert
- **1980**: Featured in Warren Beatty's film *Reds*

## Arts Centre Era

- **2000**: Opened as Zion Arts Centre, a multi-arts facility for young people
- **2011**: Celebrated the building's centenary with community engagement programmes
- **2012**: Rebranded as "Z-arts"

Since opening, the venue has provided arts-based cultural activities and hosted performances by notable artists including Damien Hirst and Courtney Pine.
