**First Street** is a major mixed-use development on the former Gaythorn Gas Works site, transforming a contaminated industrial brownfield into one of Manchester's most vibrant cultural destinations.

## Timeline

- **2010**: Number One First Street opened, marking the start of the neighbourhood's transformation
- **2015**: HOME arts centre opened (£25 million)
- **2016**: The Gasworks Brewbar opened, named after the site's industrial heritage
- **2017**: Friedrich Engels statue unveiled at Tony Wilson Place
- **2021**: Homeground outdoor summer festival during COVID lockdowns
- **2023**: Square Gardens £400 million development announced

## Square Gardens

In May 2023, a £400 million mixed-use development called **Square Gardens** was unveiled:

- **1,772 apartments** across three tower blocks
- **17,000 sq ft** of commercial, leisure, and retail space
- **9,900 sq m** of new landscaping with over 140 new trees
- Designed by **Simpson Haugh Partnership**, developed by **Downing**
- Named after Garden Street, which previously occupied the site

## Cultural Hub

First Street is now home to:

- **HOME** - arts centre with five cinemas, two theatres, and gallery space (3 million+ visitors since opening)
- Restaurants including Indian Tiffin Room and WOOD by MasterChef winner Simon Wood
- The Gasworks Brewbar with on-site brewery
