## New Event Ideas (From Research)

### LGBT History
- **Joyce Layland** (1933-2006) - Activist the Sidney Street centre is named after
- **Section 28 Protests** (1988) - National resistance headquartered at Bloom Street
- **Mancunian Gay Magazine** (1980) - First issues produced at Waterloo Place centre

### Black History
- **Elouise Edwards** - Photographer and Abasindi co-founder, photo collection at Race Archive
- **Kath Locke** (1948-2005) - Abasindi founder, Kath Locke Centre named after her
- **Manchester Black Women's Co-operative** (1975-1979) - Predecessor to Abasindi

### Arts & Culture
- **Cornerhouse** (1985-2015) - Arts cinema on Oxford Street, merged into HOME
- **Library Theatre Company** (1952-2015) - Repertory theatre, merged into HOME
- **Hulme Playhouse / BBC Playhouse** (1902-1986) - Theatre that became Nia Centre
- **Factory Records in Hulme** - Pre-Haçienda gigs in the area

### Infrastructure & Industry
- **Dunlop Rubber Works** - Industrial site, now First Street
- **Manchester Ship Canal** (1894) - Major engineering project
- **Pomona Docks** - Historic docks area

### Housing & Planning
- **Hulme V Replan** - Community alternative to City Challenge
- **Hulme Views Project** (1990-1991) - Produced "Views from the Crescents" book

### People
- **Emmeline Pankhurst** - Born in Moss Side, connections to area
- **LS Lowry** - Painted Hulme scenes

### Recent History
- **2002 Commonwealth Games** - Impact on area (draft exists)
- **Red Bricks Cinema** - Community cinema (draft exists)
- **Crucifix for a Tree** (2005) - Public art (draft exists)
